Personal statement

I have always had a deep passion for mathematics and am particularly drawn to formal logic, set theory, and complexity theory. I am currently pursuing a PhD in theoretical computer science at UC San Diego studying complexity theory and its applications to machine learning and cryptography. I am deeply interested in the limits of different computational models as well as the conditions sufficient to prove a complexity class can be learned or contains pseudorandomness. My long term goal is to work at a university or a research lab making significant contributions to the areas of complexity theory and algorithm design.
